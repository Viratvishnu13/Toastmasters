This Week's Focus (Sep 17, 2025)

Every meeting has a unique theme, a new word, and a phrase to expand our vocabulary.

Theme of the Day

"The Art of Storytelling"

This week, we explore how weaving narratives into our speeches can make them more memorable, persuasive, and engaging for any audience.

Word of the Day

Anecdote

[an-ik-doht]

A short, amusing or interesting story about a real incident or person.

Usage: "He opened his speech with a humorous anecdote about his first day at work."

Phrase of the Day

Break the ice

To initiate conversation in a social setting and make people feel more comfortable.

Usage: "A fun fact from each person is a great way to break the ice."

A Glimpse Into Our Assembly

A Toastmasters meeting is not a lecture; it's a dynamic, hands-on workshop.

1. Prepared Speeches

Members deliver 5-7 minute speeches based on projects from their Pathways learning path, practicing skills like storytelling, research, or persuasive speaking.

2. Table Topics® (Impromptu Speaking)

Members and willing guests are invited to give a 1-2 minute impromptu speech on a given topic. It’s fun, challenging, and builds quick-thinking skills.

3. Evaluations (The Gift of Feedback)

This is where the magic happens. Every speaker receives immediate, constructive feedback from an assigned evaluator, focusing on strengths and suggestions for improvement.

Our Meeting Flow & Roles Explained

Each meeting is a structured journey of learning and practice, where every role matters.

10:30 AM

SAA Begins the Meeting

Significance: The Sergeant at Arms sets a professional tone, ensuring an on-time start.
Skills Learned: Punctuality, event management, creating a positive atmosphere.

10:33 AM

Presidential Address

Significance: The President welcomes all, shares club news, and inspires the audience.
Skills Learned: Formal address, leadership communication, motivation.

10:34 AM

Secretary Reads MoM

Significance: Reading the Minutes of the previous Meeting ensures accurate record-keeping.
Skills Learned: Active listening, concise writing, formal reporting.

10:36 AM

TMOD Takes Control

Significance: The Toastmaster of the Day (TMOD) is the host, guiding the entire meeting.
Skills Learned: Meeting facilitation, time management, improvisation.

10:40 AM

GE & TAG Team Introduction

Significance: The General Evaluator, Timer, Ah-Counter, & Grammarian explain their feedback roles.
Skills Learned: Critical observation, active listening, time and language analysis.

10:45 AM

Prepared Speeches

Significance: Members deliver speeches from their educational paths to practice specific skills.
Skills Learned: Speech writing, confidence, persuasion, storytelling.

11:05 AM

Table Topics Session

Significance: An exciting impromptu speaking session to practice thinking on your feet.
Skills Learned: Structuring thoughts quickly, spontaneous speaking.

11:35 AM

General Evaluation

Significance: The core feedback part of the meeting for all speakers and role-players.
Skills Learned: Constructive feedback, critical analysis, motivational speaking.

11:43 AM

TAG Team Reports

Significance: The Timer, Ah-Counter, and Grammarian present their objective findings.
Skills Learned: Data presentation, concise reporting.

11:58 AM

Wrap-up & Closing

Significance: The TMOD summarizes and the President gives closing remarks and awards.
Skills Learned: Summarization, meeting closure, recognizing achievements.
